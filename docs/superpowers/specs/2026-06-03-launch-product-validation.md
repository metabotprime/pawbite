# PawBite — Launch Product Validation (Demand Research)

**Date:** 2026-06-03
**Question asked:** Do our two launch SKUs (Daily Probiotic + Hip & Joint) actually have high demand, or did we pick wrong?
**Verdict:** **Keep Daily Probiotic + Hip & Joint.** Confirmed for a DTC-subscription model, with roadmap refinements below.

---

## TL;DR

- **Daily Probiotic** is the unambiguous #1 launch SKU — it wins on every data lens (market $, unit volume, winnable demand). No doubt.
- **Hip & Joint** is the right #2 **for PawBite's specific model** (subscription-first, premium DTC, content/SEO-led, margin-optimized). It is the #1 category *by dollars* with the best subscription LTV.
- The research surfaced legitimate alternatives (Omega, Multivitamin) that win on *unit volume* and *winnability* respectively — but they optimize for Amazon, not for our DTC-subscription margin/LTV model.
- **The one fork:** if go-to-market is Amazon-first/volume-first, the #2 flips to **Omega/fish oil**. Confirm channel assumption.

---

## Three data lenses (they disagree — that's the insight)

| Lens | Measures | Winning pair |
|---|---|---|
| Market $ / category size | Revenue + growth | **Probiotic + Hip & Joint** (our pick) |
| Competitor best-sellers | Actual unit volume (review counts) | Probiotic + Omega/fish oil |
| Winnable demand | Demand minus incumbent lock-in | Probiotic + Multivitamin (Calming as alt) |

**Probiotic wins all three.** The #2 slot is genuinely contested.

## Why Hip & Joint stays as #2 (not Omega or Multivitamin)

PawBite optimizes **margin × LTV × brand coherence**, not Amazon units:

- **Omega** = highest units but $15–25 commodity, thin margin, zero differentiation → wrong for a premium subscription brand. (Correct call: keep omega *inside* the Hip & Joint chew, don't sell standalone at launch.)
- **Multivitamin** = biggest "winnable aggregator," but it IS the 15-SKU junk drawer PawBite positions *against* (vs Zesty). Lower margin, weaker hero-benefit story. The closest call — a conscious "focus over aggregator" choice.
- **Calming** = best search intent + least locked, but episodic/seasonal demand (fireworks, storms, travel) = weaker subscription retention than a daily necessity. **Best #3, not #2.**
- **Hip & Joint** = #1 by dollars, highest AOV ($38 vs omega $15–25), chronic daily lifelong repeat (best LTV), clean "gut + mobility" story, winnable in the DTC chew channel (Cosequin locks the *vet* channel, not DTC/Amazon chews), real 6-active wedge vs Cosequin's 2.

## Refinements the data demands

1. **Roadmap, locked:** SKU #3 = **Calming** (strongest winnable demand + search intent). SKU #4 = **Omega / Skin & Coat** (huge units; monetizes skin-coat/omega search traffic our content already ranks for). Both moved *up* from prior vague roadmap.
2. **Sharpen the Hip & Joint anti-Cosequin wedge** on the PDP (6 actives vs 2, DTC convenience, subscription price). `/vs/cosequin` page already does this — mirror it on the PDP.
3. **Vindicated "skip" calls:** Dental (thin supplement niche; real money in Greenies-owned chews), CBD, cat — all correctly deferred.
